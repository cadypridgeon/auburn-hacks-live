Projects

Hacking begins Saturday, February 11th at 1 pm and ends Sunday, February 12th at 11 am. Judging begins Sunday, February 12th at 1 pm.

DEVPOST

Project submissions will take place on Devpost. To successfully submit your project on time, please follow the directions below:

  1. Create a DevPost account.
    • You can sign up with GitHub, Google, Facebook, LinkedIn, or email.
  2. Register for Auburn Hacks.
    • Click “Join hackathon” button on the Auburn Hacks 2023 DevPost page.
    • Fill out the registration form and click “Register”.
  3. Submit your project.
    • Note: You must submit your project by the soft deadline (2/12/23 at 11:00 AM).
      • You may continue to edit your DevPost submission until the hard deadline (2/12/23 at 12:00 PM).
    • Include the following in your submission:
      • List all team members (1-4 people).
      • Title your project.
      • Write a brief description of your project.
      • Provide a link to your project repository on GitHub.
      • Select any opt-in prizes you would like your project to be considered for. If you do not opt-in, you will not be considered for that prize.
    • If you encounter any issues, feel free to send a question or concern in the #ask-staff channel on Discord.
  4. Make sure your project meets the Auburn Hacks 2023 Rules found in the Notion page and MLH Code of Conduct.
    • If your project or team does not meet our guidelines, your project will be disqualified.

Questions

Can I attend virtually?

We are not holding the hackathon virtually this year. Everyone who participates must be in-person.

What should I bring to the hackathon?

We recommend bringing a laptop, mouse/mousepad, additional monitors, chargers, headphones/earbuds, deodorant, contacts/glasses, skincare, toothpaste/toothbrush/retainers, and a sleeping bag/blanket/pillow.

What should I wear to the hackathon?

We encourage everyone to show up in their favorite tropical attire this year! Hawaiian shirts are highly recommended.

Where should I park?

Please park in the “A” zone parking lots beside the 320 West Mag apartment complex (310W W Magnolia Ave, Auburn, AL 36832) or in front of Lowder (422 War Eagle Way, Auburn, AL 36849).

Where do I go to check in?

Check-in will be at the top of the main staircase to the second floor in the Brown-Kopel Engineering Student Achievement Center. There will be staff at a table ready to check you in!

I’m going to be late! Where do I go?

There will be staff checking in participants at the second floor of Brown-Kopel until 12:00 PM. If you arrive after then, please let us know on Discord in the #general channel.

I don’t have a team. How do I make one?

No worries! We have a social hour between check-in and the opening ceremony for you to socialize with potential teammates. After the opening ceremony, there will be a team formation event for you to officially create your team!

How many people can be on a team?

There can be up to four people on a team. No exceptions.

Where can I see the schedule?

You can see the schedule on the live site, the Notion main page, and on the shared Google Calendar. We will also send event reminders in Discord on the #announcements channel.

How do I join the Discord?

You can join the Discord at aub.ie/hacksdiscord

Can I leave Brown-Kopel?

Yes. Brown-Kopel’s doors automatically lock over the weekend, so when you need to reenter, please send a message in the #general Discord channel.

HELP! I’m locked out of Brown-Kopel! What do I do?

Please send a message in the #general Discord channel. A staff member will let you in ASAP.

How do I get technical help with my project?

Please send a message in the #ask-staff Discord channel. A staff member will help you or provide you resources ASAP.

How do I submit my project?

Use DevPost to submit your project by the Project Submission Deadline (2/12/23 at 11:00 AM). Please go to the live site or appropriate Notion page for more information.

CHALLENGES

MongoDB Atlas

Best Use of MongoDB Atlas

MongoDB Atlas takes the leading modern database and makes it accessible in the cloud! Get started with a $50 credit for students or sign up for the Atlas free forever tier (no credit card required). Along with a suite of services and functionalities, you'll have everything you need to manage all of your data! Build a hack using MongoDB Atlas for a chance to win a M5GO IoT Starter Kit for you and each member of your team.

Prize: M5G0 IoT Starter Kit

Domain.com

Best Domain Name from Domain.com

Register a .tech domain name using Domain.com during the weekend. Each team may submit one entry per person on the team. Each winning team member will receive a Domain.com Branded Backpack.

Prize: Domain.com Branded Backpacks

GitHub

Most Creative Use of GitHub

GitHub is one of the best ways to collaborate, push code, get feedback, and show the world what you’ve built during a hackathon. To take it a step further, GitHub is now offering you access to industry tools, events & learning resources through something called GitHub Global Campus. Win this weekend’s Most Creative Use of GitHub prize category, first by signing up for GitHub Global Campus and second by using a GitHub repository to host your hackathon project’s code! Make sure your use of GitHub stands out with a detailed ReadMe page, meaningful pull requests and collaboration history, and even a GitHub pages deployment!

Prize: GitHub Octocat Puzzle & Sticker bundle

Twilio

Most Creative Use of Twilio

Twilio allows you to incorporate mobile messaging, phone calls and a ton of other awesome communication features right into your hackathon project using web service APIs. Are you building an e-commerce website and want to send text notifications or email confirmations once an order is completed? Or maybe your application needs to verify users based on their mobile numbers? Twilio makes all this possible and more. Build a hack that simplifies your life using any one of Twilio’s APIs for a chance to win a Twilio Swag Box and GameGo Console for you and each of your teammates!

Prize: GameGo Console & Twilio Swag Bag

Fidelity

Best Accessibility Hack

At Fidelity, accessibility is viewed as a major key to the success of their business, which is why they are challenging you to come up with innovative ways to make tech more accessible! We want you to use your hacking skills to find and build solutions that put opportunities and resources within reach to all communities. The team with the best accessibility hack will win a Fidelity branded wireless charger for each team member!

Prize: Fidelity Branded Wireless Charger

Southwest Research Institute

Southwest Research Institute are R&D problem solvers providing independent, premier services to government and industry clients. Their multidisciplinary nature allows them to rapidly assemble diverse teams to tackle problems from multiple directions. They push the boundaries of science and technology to develop innovative solutions that advance the state of the art and improve human health and safety. Operating as a nonprofit since their 1947 inception, they work in the public’s best interest and toward the betterment of humanity.

McLeod Software

McLeod Software is based in Birmingham, AL and has been serving the trucking industry for over 35 years. If you are a software professional, come join us. If you are a trucking carrier, broker, 3PL, or logistics services provider, let us show you how we can add value for your business.
